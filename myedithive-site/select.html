The selects pass is where good editing days go to die.

Hours before the real work starts

Hundreds of clips land. You scrub every one before a single creative decision gets made.

Fatigue misses the keepers

By clip 200 your eyes glaze. Three hours in, the best take of the day is easy to skip.

Every project, the same grind

Selects don't compound. Last week's hours buy you nothing on the next job. Pure repetition.

One pass in. Four timelines out.

Primary selects

The strongest moments from every take, trimmed clean and in order. The timeline you start cutting from.

Alternates

The close calls. When two readings both work, the runner-up waits here instead of disappearing.

Grouped

Footage organised by scene and subject. Finding every shot of one moment takes seconds.

Residual

Everything cut, kept and labelled. Disagree with the machine and pull a moment back.

How it works

  1. 01

    Give it a source

    A footage folder, a Final Cut Pro XML, or your open DaVinci Resolve timeline. Pick a project type and quality mode.

  2. 02

    Six stages, on your machine

    Ingest, decode, frame scoring, grouping, ranking, export. Focus, motion, composition and performance scored frame by frame. The dead parts inside each take get trimmed. Nothing is uploaded.

  3. 03

    Four ranked timelines come back

    Selects, ready to cut. Alternates, the close calls. Grouped, by scene and subject. Residual, everything it removed. Nothing is ever lost.
